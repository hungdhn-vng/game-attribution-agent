The model only narrates — and the citation validator is a hard gate: any claim without a ledger citation never ships. It routes, maps columns, and writes prose. It does not do the math.

Layer four. Most tools give you one number. We give two: how likely is this cause, and how good is the evidence behind it. Every claim sits on this likelihood-by-evidence grid, so a confident guess and a well-supported finding never look the same.

The ethos

Scenarios, not decisions.

The human decides.

Layer five, the one I'm proudest of: when the evidence is thin, it says so. It lists its assumptions and gaps and lowers its own confidence. A self-consistency gate and the citation validator reject shaky claims. It gives you scenarios, not decisions — the human still decides.
